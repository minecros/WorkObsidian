A **Virtual Private Network (VPN)** provides a **secure, encrypted connection** over the Internet between your device and another network, protecting your data and privacy. These often provide remote offices or individual users with secure access to their proprietary data.
A **VPN appliance** is a **network device equipped with enhanced security features.** Also known as an SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) VPN appliance, it is in effect a router that provides firewall protection, load balancing, authorization, authentication, and encryption for VPNs.
An **effective VPN appliance** offers:
- centralized management - allows easy configuration, and updating of VPN settings across multiple devices from a single interface
- multi-platform functionality - Works across a range of operating systems and devices
- network compatibility - supports network applications, industrial protocols and legacy infrastructure, ensuring seamless integration with control system
**TLS VPN (Transport Layer Security)** is a commonly used protocol for managing the security of message transmission on the Internet via the web browser. It allows secure remote access through standard HTTPS connections when client-side installation is limited.
TLS VPN is **suited for industrial portals or control interfaces** with minimal configuration.

**VPNs connect two or more Local Area Networks (LANs)** across the public internet (e.g. office in New York and office in London).
They **create an encrypted tunnel between VPN gateways at each location,** allowing devices on different networks to communicate as if they were on the same private network.
The two **endpoints of the VPN are intermediary devices** that pass traffic from one trusted network to another while relying on the VPN technology to secure the traffic on the untrusted transport network.

**Remote Access Service (RAS)** **connects a single user or device to a company network from any location.**
**RAS** is often used by remote workers to securely access files, apps, etc., and **requires VPN software or an app** that the user logs into to connect to the network.
One endpoint is a host computing device, and the other is an intermediate device that passes traffic from the host to the trusted network behind the gateway, relying on the VPN technology to secure the traffic on the untrusted network.
