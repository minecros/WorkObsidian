# Overview
## NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- Framework Core (Outcomes)
	- Taxonomy of outcomes written in clear, common language accessible to all audiences
	- Organized into a hierarchy of 6 Functions, 23 Categories, and 108 Subcategories that is designed to complement existing risk management practices
	- Sector, country, and technology-neutral, providing the flexibility needed to address an organization's unique risks, technologies, and mission considerations
- Organizational Profiles (Alignment with Organizational Need)
	- Describes how an organization's goals, risk tolerance and resources align with the CSF outcomes
	- Establishes a current or target cybersecurity posture to identify and prioritize opportunities for improving security standards and reducing risk
- Tiers (Rigor of Risk Management)
	- Determines the level of cybersecurity maturity based on risk appetite, mission priorities and budget alignment
## Functions
1. Identify - Establish an organizational profile of systems, assets, and risk to prioritize cybersecurity efforts..
2. Protect - Implement safeguards to prevent or limit the impact of potential cyber incidents.
3. Detect - Enable timely discovery of breaches through continuous monitoring and threat detection.
4. Respond - Develop techniques to contain and mitigate the effects of detected incidents.
5. Recover - Restore affected systems and capabilities while improving resilience for future incidents.
6. Govern - Create risk management strategy, policies and oversight mechanism to align with business goals and ensure accountability.

## Implementation Tiers
Describes the maturity level of an organization's cybersecurity practice and their level of integration and adaptation across the organization
The higher the tier, the more compliant you are with the framework
1. Tier 1 - Partial - Informal, reactive or nonexistent, with limited awareness of risks and ad hoc, inconsistent practices
2. Tier 2 - Risk Informed - Isolated implementation with some risk management processes defined but not fully integrated across the organization
3. Tier 3 - Repeatable - Formalized and structured risk management processes that are proactive, documented and regularly evauluated for efffectiveness.
4. Tier 4 - Adaptive - Responsive risk management programs based on predictive indicators that are continuously adopted and improved to address evolving risks and changing business needs.
## ISA/IEC 62443 Mapping to NIST CSF Elements
Mapping ISA/IEC 62443 (Parts 2-1, 3-2, and 3-3) to the NIST CSF leverages the strengths of both standards for a more comprehensive cybersecurity approach.
This enables organizations to integrate ICS security practices with a broader, more robust cybersecurity risk management framework.
It also offers the flexibility to tailor the mappings to an organization's specific needs, depending on its industry, size, and risk profile.
