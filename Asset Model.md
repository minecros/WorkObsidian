Defines the relationships between assets within an IACS.
An **asset model** is a **centralized framework** used to **identify, categorize, and manage the assets of the IACS**.
**Assets are the physical and logical components** (e.g. machines, sensors, control systems, networks and ancillary equipment, etc.)
An asset model **provides a clear overview of the entire system** allowing stakeholders to track the status, location, function and relationship of each asset.
It must start at a high level and be generic enough to fit the many situations and industry segments where control systems are deployed.

**Real-life examples of how asset models are used:**
- Cataloging each piece of equipment ensures a comprehensive inventory, aiding in effective tracking and management of assets. - equipment inventory
- Prioritizing security measures helps mitigate risks and enhances the overall safety and resilience of the system. - Security Prioritization
- Managing asset lifecycles optimizes maintenance schedules, reduces downtime, and extends the operational life of equipment. - Asset Lifecycle Management

This is a **SCADA example** of an asset model. It illustrates **ancillary information systems at various hierarchy levels, interacting with control equipment.** The **systems** collect data and send process instructions, but they **do not directly control the processes.** Line, area, and site **systems store and serve production data** to **users** across the **enterprise**. This model can be collapsed or expanded to focus on specific areas while staying consistent with other models and views.
